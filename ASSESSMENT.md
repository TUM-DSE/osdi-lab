# OSDI-LAB Assessment -- Winter 2026

During this course students will be assessed on a the project milestones and the final report and presentation.

## Grade Calculation

The final grade is the weighted average of individual and group milestones, final report, and final presentation.

 * **Individual Grades (25%)**
   - 10% Milestone Presentations (M0, M1)
   - 10% Milestone Integration Tests (M7)
   - 5% Report (M7)
 * **Group Grades (75%) **
   - 25% Milestone Presentations (M2, M3, M4, M5, M6)
   - 30% Milestone Integration Tests (M1--M6)
   - 10% Report (M1--M6)
   - 10% Class Presentation

The course staff reserves the right to conduct additional assessments, interviews, or scale the grade if a team member's contributions are deemed disproportionally below or above.

## Milestones

Milestones build upon each other. To help with prioritization of the milestone tasks, we distinguish between:

 - Base tasks: minimum requirement. Later milestoens depend on this functionality.
 - Target tasks: target functionality for a more complete implementation.
 - Extra Challenges: Optional. Not required for full marks.

To pass the course, participants and teams must successfully complete each milestone's base tasks.

Extra challenges only earn bonus points iff and only iff the base and target tasks are completed successfully.


## Deadlines and Late Submissions

The deadlines for the course milestones are **hard deadlines**. Missing a deadline counts as late submission.

Late submissions will be graded at 75% (one week delay) and 50% (two weeks delay).

A late submission cannot earn points for extra challenges.


## Milestone Presentations

Participents present each milestone to a member of the course staff.
We expect that each participant is able to demonstrate that they have implemented the required functionality and be able to answer questions about their solution.
Failure to answer questions or demonstrate the required functionality will result in a lower milestone grade.

For group milestones, each member must be able to explain the implementation and design decisions made by the group.

At a minimum, participants and teams must successfully and convincingly demonstrate that their implementation meets the base requiremements for the milestone.
